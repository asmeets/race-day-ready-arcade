Use this section to keep the experience readable, inclusive, and reliable in classrooms and live events.

## Readability and Clarity

- keep instructions short and visible on the projector or shared display
- introduce one mechanic at a time before layering in strategy discussion
- use simple, device-neutral action words such as select, choose, and press
- make the next useful action clear after a test, mistake, or reset

## Multiple Cues

- support key moments with more than one cue when possible, such as motion, score, text, and sound
- avoid relying on color alone to communicate success, failure, or urgency
- keep status text large enough to read from the back of the room

## Participation Supports

- pair learners so one person drives navigation and one person explains observations
- provide sentence starters when teams need support describing tradeoffs or evidence
- pause for a compare-and-explain moment before independent remixing if the room needs a reset

## Reliability Notes

- keep a fallback demo ready in case a learner device stalls or loses connection
- choose shared-device roles that are easy to explain and rotate
- prefer short retry loops so mistakes feel recoverable instead of punishing
