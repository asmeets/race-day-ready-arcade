This section outlines the detailed facilitation moves for a classroom lesson, workshop, or live activation.

## Recommended 45-60 Minute Flow

### 1. Launch the Challenge

- introduce the idea that racing is full of tradeoffs, not just speed
- show the skillmap and tell learners they will build, test, and improve
- preview one career connection such as software engineer, test engineer, or strategist

### 2. Guided Build Time

- let learners move through the skillmap in sequence
- pause after a visible mechanic change so teams can compare outcomes
- keep directions short and concrete

### 3. Compare and Explain

Use the session test track or another quick replay moment to ask what changed and why.

- What did you change?
- What improved?
- What became harder to manage?

### 4. Reflect and Connect

- connect player choices to engineering roles and team collaboration
- ask learners which tradeoff felt most important in their design
- end with one takeaway about testing, iteration, or sustainability

## Facilitator Moves That Help

- point to one variable or block at a time
- ask learners to predict before they test
- treat unexpected results as useful data
- keep discussions grounded in what the game actually showed

## Tips for Live Events

- use shorter rounds with faster resets
- prioritize highly visible changes over long explanations
- keep a fallback demo ready in case connectivity is uneven
