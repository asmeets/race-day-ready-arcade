Driven by STEM is a MakeCode Arcade learning experience built for the F1 x Mercedes x Microsoft STEM and career exploration activation. It is designed to feel like a short, high-energy invitation into engineering thinking: students make a choice, test it, notice what changed, and talk about why it changed.

The experience is more than a racing game and more than a coding lesson. It is a guided pathway that uses gameplay, reflection, and remixing to help learners see that engineering is about balancing priorities, responding to feedback, and improving a design over time.

## The Partnership

Driven by STEM brings together three organizations with a shared interest in performance, problem-solving, and future talent.

### Formula 1

Formula 1 provides the real-world context that makes tradeoffs visible. Every race depends on decisions about speed, reliability, timing, data, and teamwork.

### Mercedes-AMG Petronas Formula One Team

Mercedes helps anchor the project in the reality of a modern team, where engineering, software, strategy, testing, and sustainability all shape what happens on track.

### Microsoft

Microsoft MakeCode Arcade provides the classroom-friendly platform. It keeps the entry point accessible for beginners while still leaving room for remixing, experimentation, and deeper technical thinking.

## What Learners Experience

The skillmap is structured as a three-stage journey.

- **The Garage** introduces the mission, the junior innovation team role, and the first setup tradeoffs.
- **On the Road** turns those setup choices into active gameplay, changing conditions, and career-connected pit stop moments.
- **The Finish Line** brings everything together in a final challenge, then slows down just enough for reflection and a positive close.

Across the full path, students are asked to balance three ideas rather than optimize only one:

- performance
- efficiency
- strategy

That combination is what makes the project teachable. Students can see that the fastest option is not always the strongest overall decision.

## Why Racing Works

Racing gives educators a concrete way to talk about engineering without making the session feel abstract.

- **Tradeoffs are visible:** students can feel the difference between raw speed, clean control, and long-run efficiency.
- **Feedback is immediate:** a setup change shows up quickly in movement, score, collisions, timing, or player confidence.
- **Roles make sense:** software engineers, strategists, telemetry analysts, designers, and sustainability leads all have a believable place in the conversation.
- **Iteration feels natural:** test runs, shakedowns, pit stops, and changing conditions all support a build-test-improve rhythm.

## Who It Is For

The experience is designed primarily for middle school learners, especially students who may be new to coding or still deciding whether technical work feels like it is for them. It also works well in mixed-age classrooms, workshops, and public activations where some participants are ready to jump in immediately and others need a clearer on-ramp.

Educators can use it in several formats:

- a 45-60 minute classroom session
- a shorter museum or event-floor demo
- a longer facilitated activation with more time for remixing and discussion

## What Makes the Project Classroom-Ready

- It runs in the browser and launches through a single skillmap link.
- It uses MakeCode Arcade conventions that are easy to open, explain, and remix.
- It keeps controls simple and feedback visible enough for projector use.
- It treats mistakes as useful information instead of hard failure.
- It gives facilitators specific moments to pause, compare outcomes, and connect gameplay to real work.
