Use this section to connect the experience to standards conversations without turning the session into compliance-first paperwork.

## Standards Alignment Areas

### Computer Science

- supports cause-and-effect reasoning through code changes and testing
- supports debugging, iteration, and program refinement
- supports discussion about how software systems respond to input and design choices

### Engineering and Design

- highlights tradeoffs between performance, control, efficiency, and sustainability
- asks learners to test, compare, revise, and justify design decisions
- makes evidence-based improvement part of the main activity

### Digital Literacy and Communication

- gives learners structured ways to explain what changed and why
- supports collaborative decision making in pairs or teams
- connects technical choices to audience, usability, and clarity

## Practical Fit

This experience works best when standards are used to frame reflection, design reasoning, and communication rather than to interrupt the test-and-improve loop.

## Facilitation Note

If you need to document standards alignment for planning, keep the live session focused on play, evidence, and discussion. Do the mapping in your prep notes, not in front of learners.
