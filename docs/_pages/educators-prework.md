Use this section to confirm the session has what it needs before learners arrive.

## Core Requirements

### Devices and Access

- open the skillmap link and confirm it loads in your target browser
- test audio, if your session will rely on game sound cues
- confirm input devices work the way learners will use them
- check that pop-up blockers or sign-in prompts will not slow down the start

### Room and Delivery

- queue the skillmap on the projector or facilitator machine
- decide whether learners will work solo, in pairs, or in small teams
- identify where you want to pause for discussion or compare results
- prepare one short explanation for what an engineering tradeoff means

### Materials and Prep

- have internet-connected devices ready with a modern browser
- prepare a shared display if you plan to demo controls or compare outcomes together
- keep the skillmap link and repository link easy to access during the session
- decide whether you want note-catchers, reflection slips, or no printed materials at all

### Facilitator Readiness

- skim the [Summary]({{ site.baseurl }}/educators/summary/) and [Goals]({{ site.baseurl }}/educators/goals/) pages
- review the [Definitions]({{ site.baseurl }}/educators/definitions/) page and choose which terms you will teach directly
- decide whether you want to use the session test track as a live compare-and-explain moment

## Recommended Dry Run

Complete one short pass through the skillmap yourself. The goal is not mastery. The goal is to spot friction before learners do.

## If Time Is Tight

At minimum, confirm the skillmap opens, the projector is readable, and you have one discussion prompt ready for the end.
