Use this section to make sure the session can start cleanly, stay readable, and recover quickly if something small goes wrong.

## Core Requirements

### Devices and Access

- open the skillmap link on the exact browser and device learners will use
- confirm that the game loads without sign-in prompts, blocked pop-ups, or confusing permission requests
- test controller input, keyboard input, or touch input in the same way learners will use it
- check audio if you plan to rely on sound cues, but do not make audio essential to understanding the activity

### Room and Delivery Setup

- queue the skillmap on the projector or facilitator machine before learners arrive
- confirm that important text, HUD elements, and game states are readable from the back of the room
- decide whether learners will work solo, in pairs, or in small teams
- choose one or two planned pause points for compare-and-explain discussion
- prepare one short, student-friendly explanation of what a tradeoff means

### Materials and Launch Links

- have internet-connected devices ready with a modern browser
- keep the skillmap link easy to access on every device
- keep the repository link ready in case you want to show the full project or discuss remixing
- decide whether you want printed supports, projected prompts, or a fully digital flow

### Educator Readiness

- skim [Summary]({{ site.baseurl }}/educators/summary/) and [Goals]({{ site.baseurl }}/educators/goals/) so you can explain the experience in under a minute
- review [Definitions]({{ site.baseurl }}/educators/definitions/) and decide which terms you want to teach directly and which you will introduce only when they appear naturally
- read [Accessibility]({{ site.baseurl }}/educators/accessibility/) if you will be working with shared devices, projector-heavy facilitation, or a mixed-experience group
- decide whether you want to use the Garage Shakedown test track as a compare-and-explain checkpoint

## Recommended Dry Run

Run one short pass through the skillmap yourself before the session. The goal is not mastery. The goal is to notice where learners may stall, which terms need plain-language explanation, and where you want to pause the group.

During the dry run, check for these questions:

- Where will learners need the clearest instructions?
- Which stage creates the first visible success?
- Where is the best pause for a prediction or comparison?
- Which career connection feels most natural for your group?

## Fallback Plan

Have one simple backup plan ready in case devices or connectivity are uneven.

- keep one facilitator machine ready to demo a step live
- be ready to pair learners into Driver and Navigator roles on a shared device
- choose one smaller version of the session you can still deliver if time is cut short

## If Time Is Tight

At minimum:

- confirm the skillmap opens
- confirm the projector is readable
- prepare one short explanation of tradeoff
- choose one end-of-session reflection prompt
